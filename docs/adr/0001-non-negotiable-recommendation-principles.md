# ADR 0001: Non-Negotiable Recommendation Principles for Role Ranking and Explanation

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026-03-02
- **Deciders:** Career Alignment maintainers

## Context

The recommendation pipeline blends semantic role matching, skill overlap, and comparative analysis to produce ranked role recommendations. Current scoring includes `required_coverage`, `preferred_coverage`, and `expected_signal_coverage`, with a configurable signal penalty in `engine.py`. The same pipeline also generates comparator narratives and cross-role ranking outputs.

Relevant modules:

- `matching/embeddings.py`
- `matching/skill_overlap.py`
- `engine.py`
- `agents/role_comparator.py`
- `analysis/cross_role.py`

We need explicit guardrails that prevent high-confidence recommendations from being driven by generic leadership-skill overlap while domain mismatch is ignored, and we need consistent recommendation explainability outputs.

## Decision

The recommendation system adopts the following **non-negotiable principles**.

### Principle 1: Generic leadership overlap cannot dominate role ranking

Role ranking must not be primarily determined by transferable leadership skills when required-role/domain signals are missing.

**Policy interpretation**

- Generic/transferable skill matches (for example: communication, stakeholder management, leadership, coordination) may improve rank but cannot independently determine top rank.
- Required-skill and domain-signal evidence must remain rank-critical features.

### Principle 2: Domain-signals mismatch applies a structural penalty

Low `expected_signal_coverage` must produce a deterministic, structural reduction in role viability (not just a narrative warning).

**Policy interpretation**

- Roles with near-zero expected signal match must be unable to outrank roles with materially better domain-signal alignment unless required coverage advantage is substantial and explicit.
- The penalty must be visible in machine-readable outputs.

### Principle 3: Recommendation outputs must include inclusion and exclusion reasoning

Each recommended role must explain both:

1. **Why this role was considered** (inclusion rationale)
2. **Why alternatives were excluded or ranked lower** (exclusion rationale)

These explanations are mandatory output fields, not optional narrative text.

## Acceptance Criteria (Measurable)

All criteria below are evaluated from existing pipeline outputs and fields generated by the modules listed above.

### A. Rank inflation guard when `expected_signal_coverage` is near zero

**Definition:** “Near zero” means `expected_signal_coverage <= 0.10`.

For any role where `expected_signal_coverage <= 0.10`:

1. **Composite score cap:** its post-penalty ranking score must not exceed `0.65` unless `required_coverage >= 0.85`.
2. **Relative inflation cap:** compared to the same role with penalty removed, max rank-position improvement due to non-domain overlap is **0 positions** (i.e., it may not move up because of generic overlap once near-zero signal is established).
3. **Top-slot gate:** it cannot appear as rank #1 when there exists at least one other candidate role with `expected_signal_coverage >= 0.40` and `required_coverage >= 0.60`.

### B. Minimum explanation payload per recommended role

For every role in recommendation output, required explanation fields are:

- `why_considered` (non-empty string; inclusion rationale)
- `why_not_higher` (non-empty string; exclusion/comparative rationale)
- `key_evidence` (array, minimum length 2, containing concrete evidence items)

A recommendation record is non-compliant if any required field is missing, empty, or structurally invalid.

### C. Cohort regression thresholds

For recurring benchmark cohorts (`builder_like`, `clinical_pivot`, `non_profit`, `technical`), regressions are evaluated against the current accepted baseline.

1. **Ranking stability:** top-3 role set Jaccard similarity must be `>= 0.67` per cohort.
2. **Domain-mismatch containment:** number of top-3 roles with `expected_signal_coverage <= 0.10` must not increase by more than `+0` versus baseline.
3. **Explanation completeness:** 100% of top-3 recommendations per cohort must include all required explanation fields in Criterion B.
4. **Penalty efficacy:** for any cohort with at least one near-zero-signal role in top-5, that role’s penalized score must be at least `0.05` lower than its unpenalized overlap score.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Reduces false-positive recommendations caused by generic skill transfer alone.
- Makes domain mismatch impact explicit and auditable.
- Increases recommendation transparency for user trust and debugging.

### Trade-offs

- Some cross-domain pivots may be ranked more conservatively.
- Additional output-schema requirements may require updates where recommendations are serialized.

## Implementation Notes

- `matching/skill_overlap.py` remains the source for `required_coverage`, `preferred_coverage`, and `expected_signal_coverage`.
- `engine.py` is the enforcement point for structural penalty and rank gating behavior.
- `agents/role_comparator.py` and/or downstream output assembly should emit required explanation fields.
- `analysis/cross_role.py` can be used to validate ranking movement and regression checks at cohort level.
- `matching/embeddings.py` remains the role/profile semantic signal substrate and should continue exposing expected-signal context.

## Compliance Checkpoints

A change is ADR-compliant only if:

1. The scoring path enforces Criteria A.
2. The recommendation schema enforces Criteria B.
3. Regression evaluation for the four cohorts enforces Criteria C.
